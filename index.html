Tribute of Nelson Mandela

The man who freed the south africans from the chains of the apartheid
Nelson Mandela receiving the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.
Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:
  • 1918 - Born Rolihlahla Mandela at Mvezo in the Transkei
  • 1925 - Attends primary school near Qunu (receives the name ‘Nelson’ from a teacher)
  • 1930 - Father dies. Entrusted to Thembu Regent Jongintaba Dalindyebo at the age of 12
  • 1940 - Expelled
  • 1941 - Escapes an arranged marriage; becomes a mine security officer; starts articles at the law firm Witkin, Sidelsky & Eidelman
  • 1960 - Sharpeville Massacre
  • 1962 - Sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1990 - Released
  • 1993 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk for his work for the peaceful termination of the apartheid regime, and for laying the foundations for a new democratic South Africa
  • 1994 - Votes for the first time in his life and becomes president of the Republic of South Africa
  • 2013 - Spends his 95th birthday in hospital. Later that year passes away at home in Johannesburg
